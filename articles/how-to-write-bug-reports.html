Why bad bug reports get ignored

Most bug reports are not ignored because developers do not care. They are ignored because they are unclear, incomplete, or slow to verify.

  • Missing steps
  • No expected result
  • No environment context
  • No evidence

At that point, the report becomes a guessing game. Developers have to recreate the issue themselves, which wastes time.

A bad bug report creates more work. A good one removes it.

Weak vs strong QA bug report example

Weak

The game is bugged and doesn’t work after dying.

Strong

Title: Player cannot attack after respawn (PC, controller)

Steps: Respawn → press attack

Expected: Attack works

Actual: No response

Evidence: Clip attached

Key takeaways

  • Clear structure beats long explanations
  • Repro steps are the most important part
  • Evidence removes doubt
  • Good reports save developer time

FAQ

How do you write a good bug report?

Use clear steps, expected vs actual, and include evidence.

What makes a bug report useful?

Reproducibility and clarity.

Do all bug reports need video evidence?

No, but high-impact bugs benefit from it.

What is the most important part of a QA bug report?

Steps to reproduce.
