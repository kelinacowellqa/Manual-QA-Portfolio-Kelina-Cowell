Exploratory QA & Live Testing

This section collects exploratory QA practice and live testing work across smaller, faster-moving, and less controlled environments than my main case studies.

It includes destructive testing, game jam QA, demos, alpha and beta builds, and other short-form projects where the focus is on quick bug discovery, practical judgement, and adapting to unstable or evolving builds.

The goal here is not depth for its own sake, but evidence of consistency: regular hands-on testing, clear reporting, and QA thinking applied outside longer structured project work.

Short-form destructive testing exercise completed as part of Emerge QA's’ Game QA 101 course, included here as additional exploratory bug-hunting practice rather than a primary case study.

FAQ

How is this section different from the main portfolio?

The main portfolio focuses on larger, structured case studies with deeper documentation, stronger timeboxing, and fuller QA workflows. This section is intentionally lighter and exists to show consistency, extra repetitions, and testing in faster-moving environments.

Why include smaller practice projects at all?

Because regular QA practice matters. These entries show that I test beyond formal portfolio pieces and keep building bug-hunting, reporting, and judgment skills across different contexts.

Will every entry here have a full workbook and STAR summary?

Not necessarily. Some entries will stay lightweight by design. The point of this section is to show useful additional QA work without pretending every short-form test session is a full case study.

What counts as live testing here?

Demos, alpha builds, beta builds, game jams, and other active or limited-access environments where testing happens closer to real player conditions or in less stable development phases.
