Demos, Alphas & Betas - Live Testing Work

This page collects shorter-form QA work completed on demos, alpha builds, beta builds, limited-access tools, and other live test environments where usability, stability, and real-world behaviour matter more than polished presentation.

The focus here is on practical testing in messier conditions: identifying player-facing defects quickly, spotting friction in onboarding or progression, and documenting issues clearly enough to be actionable.

These entries are intentionally lighter than my main case studies. The goal is to show consistent hands-on QA activity, adaptable bug hunting, and useful reporting across active or evolving builds.

Live Testing Lineup

Runic Tower - Targeted QA Pass

Short targeted QA pass on a live update, focused on onboarding clarity, ability feedback, and system behaviour under overlapping mechanics.

  • Goal: Evaluate whether new and updated systems are clearly communicated to the player and behave consistently during gameplay.
  • Testing focus: Onboarding flow, ability feedback (VFX/SFX), passive vs active clarity, and system behaviour across Background and Aspect mechanics.
  • Why this matters: Shows how unclear system communication and inconsistent behaviour break player understanding, even when individual abilities appear functional.
  • Outcome: Completed targeted sessions, identified multiple system-level clarity issues, including mismatches between selection, HUD, and gameplay, and inconsistent ability assignment between Player and Tower.

Corpus Et Spiritus - Targeted QA Pass

Short targeted QA pass on a live build, focused on card resolution, input handling, and player-facing clarity under rapid interaction.

  • Goal: Validate whether card interactions remain stable under sequential, rapid, and mixed inputs, and identify failure points in input handling.
  • Testing focus: Card resolution, click vs drag interaction, rapid input pressure, and card lifecycle clarity (playable vs recyclable states).
  • Why this matters: Shows how input instability and unclear system states can reduce player control and understanding, even when core mechanics are functioning correctly.
  • Outcome: Completed 2 focused sessions, logged 1 medium-severity input handling bug, and identified card lifecycle clarity as a player-facing weakness while core systems remained stable.

Cult of PiN - Live Demo Exploratory QA

Short exploratory QA pass on a live demo build, focused on ritual clarity, overlapping systems, timing behaviour, system stacking, and progression flow under real gameplay conditions.

  • Goal: Evaluate the highest-risk areas in a system-heavy roguelike pinball demo without pretending to run full regression coverage.
  • Testing focus: Ritual communication, player-facing feedback, timing and state transitions, overlapping effects, active system stacking, and end-to-end run flow.
  • Why this matters: Shows how mechanically stable systems can still create player confusion when feedback and cause-and-effect are unclear during chaotic gameplay.
  • Outcome: Completed 5 focused sessions, logged 1 low-severity visual consistency bug, and identified clarity as the main player-facing weakness while timing, stacking, and progression remained stable.

FAQ

What would you actually test if I gave you my game?

I start with whatever you’re already having issues with, based on your own feedback or player feedback. Then I expand into the rest of the game, focusing on how it feels to play, including controls, clarity, progression, consistency, and accessibility.

Can you work on a game long-term?

Yes. I can do short targeted testing, or work with you over time as the game develops. That includes testing new features, checking fixes, improving accessibility, and helping catch issues before updates go out.

Do you test for accessibility?

Yes. I look at how playable the game is for different players, including readability, input flexibility, clarity under pressure, and whether important information is easy to understand. This helps make the game more accessible without changing the core design.

What do you deliver?

Clear, practical feedback you can act on. That includes short video clips showing the issue, simple explanations of what’s happening, and steps to reproduce it so you can fix it quickly.

How do you work with small teams or solo devs?

I keep things simple and flexible. I can fit into your workflow, use your tools, or just send you structured feedback you can work through at your own pace.

What kind of problems do you usually find?

Bugs, but also the things players struggle with. That includes unclear mechanics, confusing systems, inconsistent behaviour, and accessibility issues that make the game harder to understand or play.

Can you focus on specific parts of my game?

Yes. If you want me to focus on something specific, like combat, UI, progression, or accessibility, I can go deep on that area.
