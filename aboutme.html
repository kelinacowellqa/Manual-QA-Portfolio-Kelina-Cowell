About Kelina Cowell

I am a UK-based Junior Game QA Tester building a portfolio of structured testing work across PC, mobile, and VR.

I focus on the execution of QA: planning structured test sessions, running focused test passes, capturing clear evidence, and writing bug reports that developers can act on quickly.

My experience comes from consistent testing across a range of builds and scenarios, with emphasis on reproducible bugs, clear communication, and sound QA judgement under real testing conditions.

What you will see in my work:

  • Clear scope, environment setup, and risk focus for every test pass.
  • Practical charters and targeted test cases aligned to core systems and player-facing risk.
  • Structured bug reports with repro steps, expected vs actual results, environment details, repro rate, evidence, and player impact.

How I work

  • I define scope, risk areas, test environment, and a focused strategy before testing begins.
  • I use Jira to track test passes, manage findings, and document outcomes clearly.
  • I run timeboxed sessions using charters or targeted scenarios, focusing on high-risk areas rather than broad coverage.
  • I capture video and screenshots to support test runs and provide clear evidence for reported issues.
  • I write bug reports with clear repro steps, expected vs actual results, and supporting evidence.
  • I assess system behaviour, UX clarity, accessibility, localisation, and edge cases where they impact the player experience.

Training & Professional Development

I transitioned into the games industry after several years in ambulance control room and call centre roles, where I developed strong attention to detail, structured communication, and the ability to work under pressure. These skills now directly support my approach to testing and bug reporting.

I began this transition through the Game Academy bootcamp, where I developed a strong interest in QA and started building a portfolio through self-directed testing projects. Alongside this, I completed training in Jira (Scrum and Kanban) and user story writing to support structured testing and documentation.

While working on these projects, I consistently identified accessibility issues, shaped by my own experience with dyslexia and dyscalculia. This led to a focused interest in accessibility within QA, supported by a full scholarship for the Certified Accessible Player Experiences® (APX) Practitioner training with AbleGamers, alongside completing Microsoft Gaming Accessibility Fundamentals.

This training gave me a structured approach to accessibility-focused QA, including:

  • Clear accessibility vocabulary for communicating with designers, developers, and producers.
  • Use of Access and Challenge design patterns to identify real player barriers.
  • Evaluation of accessibility as part of overall player experience, not just feature compliance.
  • Application of accessibility principles across PC, mobile, and VR.

I also completed QA 101 - Foundations of Game QA with Emerge QA, reinforcing core fundamentals such as structured bug reporting, evidence capture, and destructive testing.

I have recently been selected for the Limit Break Mentorship Programme (2026), where I will work with an industry QA professional over six months to refine my portfolio and align it with studio expectations for junior QA roles.

My focus is integrating accessibility-focused Game QA early in development by identifying player barriers through testing and design, rather than treating accessibility as a late-stage fix.
